Extended Bio

Hey! my name is Rawsen Mitchell and I am a Mechanical Engineering student currently studying at the Wentworth Institute of Technology. I am self-motivated, adaptable, and will always find a way to get things done. I grew up in the small town of Altus, Oklahoma where I graduated from Altus High. I initially attended the University of Oklahoma for a Bachelors in Architecture. I swapped to Mechanical Engineering my second semester becuase I felt Architecture wasn't challenging enough. After spending a year at OU, I realized that I wasn't going to find the opportunities I wanted in Oklahoma. I decided to apply to colleges in Boston and New York and found that the Wentworth Institute of Technology would allow me to study at an exceptional price and give me the experience I need to succeed in the field. My current mantra is: "What's next?"

Modular Model Airplane

Goal: Create an LSA (Light-Sport Aircraft) with modular features to allow for the customization of flight characteristics. The LSA has only been through one iteration. I am currently working on improving the modularity, size, portability, and increasing the number of options for the "hot swappable" pieces.

Things I've learned:
  • Advanced SolidWorks techniques
  • Rapid-prototyping methods
  • General aerodynamics
  • 3D printing techniques

Reusable travel soap box

Goal: Create a travel case for the soap from the "Dr. Sasquatch" brand. I wanted the case to have more functionality, so I designed it with a deoderant-style mechanism to elevate the soap out of the container when needed and retract when stowed. I plan to improve upon the design with the intent of profiting from the design.

Things I've learned:
  • Advanced SolidWorks design principles
  • To conduct product testing and review
  • Advanced Cura slicing methods
  • Research and production drawing analysis
